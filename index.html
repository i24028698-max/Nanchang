The City of Heroes

Nanchang, the capital of Jiangxi Province, is a city built on the banks of the Gan River. With a history spanning over 2,200 years, it serves as a crucial hub of Chinese culture and revolution.

Known as the "City of Heroes," Nanchang witnessed the birth of the modern Chinese military and continues to thrive as a center of aviation, industry, and stunning natural beauty.

Breathtaking Landmarks

Pavilion of Prince Teng

One of the Three Great Towers of Jiangnan, immortalized in classic poetry. A masterpiece of Tang Dynasty architecture.

Star of Nanchang

One of the world's tallest Ferris wheels, offering panoramic views of the Gan River and the futuristic skyline.

Qiushui Square

Home to the largest musical fountain show in Asia, lighting up the night with spectacular colors and movements.

August 1st Uprising

In 1927, the sound of the first gunshot of the Nanchang Uprising echoed across the city, marking the founding of the People's Liberation Army. This historic event forever earned Nanchang the title of "City of Heroes."
