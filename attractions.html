Iconic Landmarks

From ancient pavilions to gleaming Ferris wheels, Nanchang's landmarks tell the story of a city that honors its past while racing toward the future.

Pavilion of Prince Teng

One of the Three Great Towers of Jiangnan, this nine-story masterpiece of Tang Dynasty architecture overlooks the Gan River and has inspired poets for over 1,300 years.

Shengjin Tower

A thousand-year-old Buddhist pagoda standing seven stories tall, surrounded by vibrant temple fairs and traditional folk culture activities that bring history to life.

August 1st Uprising Museum

The birthplace of the People's Liberation Army, preserving revolutionary history since 1927.

Marquis Haihun Museum

A treasure trove of Han Dynasty relics — gold, jade, and bronze artifacts unearthed from a 2,000-year-old tomb.

Star of Nanchang

A 160-meter Ferris wheel offering breathtaking panoramic views of the city skyline, especially stunning at night.

Badashanren Memorial Hall

A tranquil memorial honoring the renowned Ming Dynasty painter Zhu Da, set within serene traditional gardens.
